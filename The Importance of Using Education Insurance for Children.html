Types of Insurance for Children
Basically, education insurance is a type of insurance that is created from the combination of two main types of insurance, namely dual-purpose insurance, and unit link insurance. But in practice, there are differences in each type of insurance, which are:

  1. Endowment Insurance is a type of insurance that has existed since time immemorial so that this type of insurance is often referred to as a type of traditional insurance. In this type of insurance does not mix investment aspects into the program so you can get a clear financial aid figure. But the education aid fund you will receive from this program will be lower than the type of insurance unit link. Gains from this type of insurance is that if the customer who asked the insurance for his children died, the policy to be free children's education fund premiums yet still continue to be given to the education of children listed in the insurance agreement.
  2. Unit link Insurance is a type of insurance that combines the insurance system with investment. So that the aid of funds that will receive far greater than the type of dual-purpose insurance. In general insurance unit link advantage of investment will you take along with the disbursement of sum insured.
