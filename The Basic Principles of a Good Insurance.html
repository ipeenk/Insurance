Insurance is basically a business or system used by certain companies where the company provides a financial protection to the customer. The protection provided is generally in the form of life, health, property, and education insurance. In the insurance system, the party who wants to get the protection is required to pay premiums regularly as one of the obligations that must be fulfilled by the customer to the company. So, nowadays many people have decided to use insurance for everything in life because it can help to plan a more orderly life and be ready for anything unexpected. Insurance itself is legally defined as an agreement whereby an insurer binds himself to an insured by accepting a premium in which the premium is used to provide reimbursement to the customer due to an adverse event such as damage, loss, death and other events. Therefore in this article will be discussed about basic principles of a good insurance in order to establish a good insurance service and guaranteed.

The Principles of an Insurance

Everyone basically needs protection where in this case, the protection is left to an insurance company that will provide financial protection. Therefore, there are several principles that must be implemented by insurance companies to be able to provide the best protection for its customers, namely:

Insurable Interest. It is a principle in which there is a right to do some insurance. The right arises from the existence of a financial relationship which occurs between the insured and the insured in which the relationship has been recognized and legally valid in a territory in which the agreement is held.

  1. Utmost Good Faith. It is an obligation of the insured candidate where the parties are obliged to disclose and provide accurate and complete information about everything that will be insured. It is intended to avoid misunderstandings between the insurer and the insured in the future when the agreement is in progress.
  2. Indemnity. It is a mechanism in which the insurer must provide financial compensation in an effort to place the insured in the financial position prior to the loss.
  3. Subrogation. It is a policy in the insurance system where the occurrence of a transfer of claim rights from the insured to the insurer immediately after the claim is paid.
  4. Contribution. It is a right held by the insurer which entitles to invite other insurers to jointly bear but the other insurer has no obligation to bear the same.
